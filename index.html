12 — Voice & Tone
How We Write
Brand Personality
🎯
Direct
Say what you mean. No filler words, no jargon, no seven-word sentences where three will do.
🤝
Empathetic
We know your days aren't perfect. We write to logistics people who've lived the chaos, not at them.
🔧
Practical
Every sentence earns its place. Specific numbers beat vague claims. Steps beat descriptions.
💪
Confident
We earned 4.8★ from real operators. We don't hedge when we know something works.
Writing Principles
✓ Do Write Like This
"Route Planning That Handles the Chaos"
Problem-first — acknowledge the reality before the solution
"Drag, drop, done."
Short declarative sentences — action verbs, concrete outcomes
"Your days aren't perfect. Neither are ours. That's why we built this."
Peer-to-peer — speak with operators, not at them
"2 hours → 10 minutes. One click."
Specific numbers — always quantify the benefit
✗ Don't Write Like This
"Our revolutionary AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge algorithms..."
Tech buzzwords — say what it does, not how it's built
"Simply utilize the interface to seamlessly optimize your routes."
Filler words — "simply", "seamlessly", "utilize" add nothing
"Empowering logistics professionals to unlock unprecedented efficiency."
Corporate-speak — no one talks like this in a dispatch office
"It may help improve certain aspects of route efficiency over time."
Hedging — if we know it works, say so confidently
Quick Rules
Headlines: Sentence case always. Never ALL CAPS in prose. Bold the value, not the product name.
Numbers: Always use digits (5 drivers, not five). Include units (28%, 48 mins, $40/mo).
CTAs: Action + outcome. "Start free trial" not "Get started". "Plan routes in 10 min" not "Learn more".
Product name: "Upper" (capitalized, never "UPPER"). "Upper Route Planner" on first mention, "Upper" after.
Sentence length: Aim for ≤20 words. Short sentences land harder in logistics contexts.
Audience address: "You" and "your team" — always second person. Never "the user" or "customers".
13 — Motion & Animation
Motion System
Principles
Purposeful
Every animation communicates something — state change, feedback, or spatial relationship. No decoration-only motion.
Subtle
Operators are focused on their work. Motion should confirm actions quickly and get out of the way.
Consistent
Same elements always animate the same way. Predictable motion builds trust with repeat users.
Duration Tokens
100ms
--dur-fast
Micro-interactions: button press feedback, checkbox toggle, badge count update
200ms
--dur-base
Default: hover states, color transitions, tooltips appear/disappear, focus rings
300ms
--dur-slow
Modals open/close, dropdowns expand, accordions, slide-in panels
500ms
--dur-enter
Page transitions, hero entrances, large content section reveals on scroll
Easing Curves
Ease Out — Entrances
cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1)
Elements entering the screen. Starts fast, decelerates to rest — feels natural and responsive.
Ease In — Exits
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)
Elements leaving the screen. Starts slow, accelerates out — doesn't linger, exits cleanly.
Ease In-Out — Interactive
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
Hover transitions, toggle states, expanding panels. Smooth in both directions.
Element-Specific Rules
Element Duration Easing Properties
Button hover 200ms ease-in-out background-color, box-shadow, transform(scale 0.98 on click)
Nav link hover 200ms ease-out color, opacity, border-bottom underline
Dropdown / Mega-menu 300ms ease-out opacity 0→1, transform translateY(-8px)→0
Modal open 300ms ease-out opacity 0→1, scale 0.95→1, backdrop-filter blur
Card hover 200ms ease-in-out box-shadow elevation increase, transform translateY(-2px)
Page section reveal 500ms ease-out opacity 0→1, transform translateY(24px)→0 on scroll enter
Toast / notification 300ms in / 200ms out ease-out / ease-in opacity + translateX(100%)→0 slide from right

20 — Product Variants
Solo vs. Crew
SOLO Individual Driver
Who: Solo couriers, small delivery operators, field service individuals.
Layout: 2-panel (left stops list + right map). No team/driver management screens.
Sidebar: Routes · Reports · Contacts · Settings
Pricing: $19.99/mo or $99.99/year · 7-day free trial
Brand accent: Cyan #06b6d4 used in marketing
Primary color: Same as Crew — #0040FF
CREW Multi-Driver Fleet
Who: Operations managers, dispatchers, fleet owners with 2–200+ drivers.
Layout: Full dashboard — topbar + sidebar + driver tracking + fleet map.
Sidebar: Routes · Drivers · Tracking · Reports · Dispatch · Settings
Pricing: From $40/mo · Per-driver pricing · Enterprise plans
Brand accent: Violet #7c3aed used in marketing
Primary color: Same as Solo — #0040FF
✓ Shared Design Language
Both products use identical design tokens — same primary color (#0040FF), same typography (Inter), same border colors, same button styles, same table patterns. The only intentional differences are the marketing accent colors (cyan for Solo, violet for Crew) and the sidebar navigation structure. Any new components built for one product should be compatible with the other.
